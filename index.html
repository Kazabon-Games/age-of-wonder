The Kingdom of Aetheria Nova

Shemsara is a city built on the principle that authority is earned, not inherited. Six noble houses each send one heir to the city at thirteen. For five years they study, build relationships, and survive a political environment where every faction has interests and every NPC has something they want in return for what they're worth.

At eighteen the crystal ceremony reveals each heir's magical school. The campaign begins the morning after. The arc one win condition is the legitimacy threshold — King Hector at +2, three factions at +2, two peer councillors at +1, one powerful endorsement at +3. Any method. The system records the score. The story records how they got there.

Getting Started — If You're a Player

Six steps. No installs, no accounts, nothing to set up beyond a web browser you already have.

  1. 1. Tap Heir Record below. Fill out all seven sections — your identity, your house, five years of study, and your key relationships. Take your time with this one; everything else builds from it.
  2. 2. At the end, tap Save Record. Your phone or computer downloads a small file — something like aow_heir_yourname.json. It goes to wherever your device normally saves downloads — usually a folder called Downloads or Files. You don't need to open it or do anything with it yet — just know it exists.
  3. 3. Send that file to your GM — drag it into Discord, email it, whatever's easiest. This is the only thing your GM needs from you before session one.
  4. 4. Come back to this page and tap Play Sheet instead.
  5. 5. Tap Import Session Zero Record. Your device will ask you to pick a file — choose the same .json file from step 2. Your whole character appears instantly.
  6. 6. That's it. Bookmark the Play Sheet page, or just remember to come back to this link. It saves everything automatically on this device, in this browser — always open it the same way and your character will be exactly how you left it.

Running the table instead? Collect each player's .json file, then open GM Screen and import all of them at once before session one.
